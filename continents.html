NORTH AMERICA

  1. Includes major countries like the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
  2. Known for natural wonders like the Grand Canyon and Niagara Falls.
  3. Home to a wide range of climates and landscapes.
  4. Economically powerful, especially the U.S.
  5. Mix of Indigenous cultures and immigrant histories.



SOUTH AMERICA

  1. Contains the Amazon Rainforest, the world's largest tropical forest.
  2. Home to the Andes, the longest continental mountain range.
  3. Rich in cultural traditions and languages, mainly Spanish and Portuguese.
  4. Famous for festivals like Carnival in Brazil.
  5. Has many natural resources like coffee, oil, and minerals.



AUSTRALIA

  1. Smallest continent and only one that is also a country.
  2. Known for unique animals like kangaroos and koalas..
  3. Includes island nations like Fiji, Samoa, and Papua New Guinea.

  5. Surrounded by the Pacific and Indian Oceans.



AFRICA

  1. Second largest continent in both size and population.
  2. Home to the Sahara Desert and the Nile River
  3. Over 50 countries with diverse cultures and languages
  4. Rich in wildlife, including lions, elephants, and giraffes.
  5. Known for natural resources like gold, diamonds, and oil..



EUROPE

  1. Known for its long history and cultural influence.
  2. Contains over 40 countries, including France, Germany, and the UK..
  3. Birthplace of the Industrial Revolution and many world-changing ideas.
  4. Features landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and Colosseum.
  5. Most countries are part of the European Union.



ASIA

  1. Largest and most populous continent.
  2. Includes China and India, the two most populous countries.
  3. Birthplace of many religions (e.g., Buddhism, Islam, Hinduism)
  4. Features diverse landscapes: mountains, deserts, forests.
  5. Leading in technology and economic growth (e.g., Japan, South Korea)



ANTARTICA

  1. Coldest, driest, and windiest continent.
  2. Covered almost entirely by ice
  3. No permanent population; only researchers stay temporarily.
  4. Penguins and seals live along its coasts.
  5. Plays a key role in global climate and sea levels.
